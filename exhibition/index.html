Entangled Growth: Networks Like Vines

Exhibition curated by Brooke Leszczynski

March 25, 2026

This exhibition explores the conceptual and visual parallels between organic growth systems—such as vines, roots, and flowers—and the structure of the internet. Like plant life, digital networks spread through branching pathways, adapting, connecting, and evolving over time. The selected artworks reflect how artists have used the web not only as a medium but as a living system that mimics ecological processes. Each work engages with ideas of connectivity, decentralization, and growth, drawing attention to how digital environments are less mechanical than they appear. Instead, they behave like ecosystems—responsive, unpredictable, and intertwined. From early net art experiments to interactive web-based environments, these pieces highlight how artists have imagined the internet as something closer to a garden than a machine. By linking natural imagery with digital structures, the exhibition situates net art within broader conversations about systems, networks, and the blurred boundary between the organic and the technological.

My Boyfriend Came Back From the War (1996)

Artist: Olia Lialina

This early net artwork uses fragmented hyperlinks that branch like vines, guiding users through a nonlinear narrative. Its structure mirrors organic growth, where each click extends the experience outward like a spreading root system.

The Garden of Forking Paths (Web Adaptations)

Artist: Inspired by Jorge Luis Borges

This interactive web-based adaptation reflects branching narrative structures similar to vines and plant systems. Each pathway diverges like a growing stem, emphasizing multiplicity and interconnected possibilities within digital space.

We Feel Fine (2006)

Artists: Jonathan Harris & Sep Kamvar

This project collects emotions from across the web and visualizes them as organic, moving forms. The interface resembles a living ecosystem, where data behaves like clusters of blooming flowers responding to human expression.
